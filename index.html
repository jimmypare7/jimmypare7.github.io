Chaos Cutting Boards

To make these cutting boards, I used maple, walnut, and cherry. To create the chaotic pattern, I start off by making a normal, long grain cutting board but then repeatedly rip the board to different widths and re-glue. Almost like making a cutting board out of a cutting board out of a cutting board. The final step is to cross cut the board and rotate the pieces such that the end grain is sticking up, revealing the pattern. End grain cutting boards are more desirable than more common long grain cutting boards because of their durability and almost 'self healing' properties. When an end grain cutting board is sliced by a knife, the gap can be closed over time as the wood naturally expands and contracts. This is because the wood expands primarily across the grain instead of with the grain.
